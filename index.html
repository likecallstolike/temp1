Quantify the Hole in My Existence

Eleven lost friends; 
I never knew what I had until it disappeared,  
Making room for uncertainty and doubt; Why  
Did I never know I had  
So much room in my 
Soul for emotion until it 
Was filled with sadness fit to drown in, 
And anger that doesn’t know a target.
 
Sixteen years,  
Nine thousand three hundred and thirty-six  
Ounces of tears;  
Thirty-seven, four hundred and sixty-four left. 
Fifth grade,  
One failed attempt in twenty-eight swallowed parts. 
It occurs to me that my closest friends 
From the past two years, 
I met over four baker acts,  
And for three days, four times, 
I felt like I could be me. 
Twelve days of friendship in the darkest of places, 
Seven hundred and eighteen days alone, 
Seventy-nine hours wishing I still knew 
Eleven lost friends; 
Zero times I told them 
How much they meant to me. 
 
I would guess 
How many times I said, “I’m okay,” 
When I wasn’t, and I would  
Factor in how many times 
I didn’t even know I wasn’t okay, 
But one-hundred and seventy-one words, 
And I’m too tired to try to quantify 
This hole in my existence 
That I don’t know how to fill, 
And I’m too lost to give this poem 
A happy ending, 
And I’m too close to that knife's edge 
That came to mind when I wrote the word,  “Ending.”
